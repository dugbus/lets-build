Wordle Golf

Wordle Golf is a fun way to play Wordle as a competition amongst friends.

The Wordle for each day becomes a hole in a round of golf with the number of guesses used to get the word counting as the number of strokes taken on the hole.

The par for each Wordle is four so guessing the word in one guess is an Albatross, scoring three under par in golf terms. Guessing the word after two tries is an Eagle, giving the player two under for the hole. Taking three guesses is a Birdie, which is one under.

Getting the word in four guesses is Par.

Five and six guesses are a Bogey and Double Bogey respectively, giving the player a score of plus one or plus two.

If you don't manage to find the word in 6 guesses you can score it as a Triple Bogey seven or award a penalty snowman, Quad Bogey for eight.

Decide what the penalty is for a missed day before you start the round.

For our inaugural match, which you can see below, we all agreed to use the same starting word, YEARS but you will probably want go your own way if you already have a favourite.

Pick a starting date and a length for your match and take a swing at Wordle Golf.
